We built our embedded spherical camera from scratch.

To begin, you center the camera over a blue dot and hover until a frame of the scene appears. Rotate your device, following the dots to capture your photosphere.

Each picture you take is attached to the corresponding section of a sphere to which your device points. When you view a photosphere in compass-mode, the inverse occursâ€”changes in your device's compass coordinates are mapped to the image rendered on the screen via linear transformations.

As UI lead, I also worked on the new user experience, log in, and intermediate views.

Photospheres are massive files (They contain XMP metadata serialized and embedded inside the image). One of the biggest challenges was creating a pleasant user experience in which views load quickly and smoothly. Parse and Square's Picasso were used to make this flow more efficient.
